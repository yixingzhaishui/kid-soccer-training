# Nolan Soccer Trainer — Skill-First Curriculum Matrix

This document is the curriculum gate. A scenario may not be animated merely because it has a different title. It must teach one observable soccer skill, begin from a valid match cue, place all 14 players in a reasonable 7-v-7 shape, and visibly prove why the best action helps the team.

Existing scenario IDs, saved scores, and approved best-strategy wording must be preserved while scenes are corrected.

## Non-negotiable teaching rules

1. A player may not receive while hidden directly behind an opponent. They must leave the opponent's cover shadow unless the lesson is specifically a timed run beyond the defensive line into a clear passing lane.
2. A good dribble may not travel through a defender. The animation must show the defender's body angle and the open inside or outside route.
3. “Make space” must visibly separate teammates into different lanes or depths. It may not mean randomly running away.
4. “Squeeze” means the connected team moves up and becomes compact after pressure, a backward pass, or a clearance. The line must not squeeze when the ball carrier has time to pass behind it.
5. “Block” means closing the ball-to-goal or ball-to-cross path with a controlled body position. It is not the same as chasing the ball.
6. “Catch” is correct only when the goalkeeper can arrive first with a stable path. Crowded or fast balls may require a punch, parry, or hold position.
7. A best option must change the team consequence: a lane opens, danger closes, support arrives, possession is protected, or a real chance is created.
8. Off-ball teammates and opponents must react during setup and consequence animations. No frozen formation players.
9. Blue always attacks the red goal; red always attacks the blue goal.
10. The highlighted area must contain the cue, Nolan's duty, and the immediate consequence—not a generic role-sized rectangle.

## Implementation gate

- [ ] Every playable scenario has an approved row in the scenario approval record.
- [ ] No best movement ends directly behind an opponent unless it is an intentionally timed run beyond a line with a clear passing lane.
- [ ] Every good and poor consequence moves at least two opponents and two teammates.
- [ ] Active-area bounds are derived from cue + duty + consequence.
- [ ] Near-duplicate detection ignores tiny coordinate changes and compares skill, geometry, ball path, and player reactions.
- [ ] A knowledgeable adult can explain the tactical reason from the animation without reading the answer text.
